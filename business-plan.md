# Console Exchange — Business Plan (v1)

*Working title — swap for your real brand/handle. Built 2026-06-28.*

---

## 0. The one-line version

> A content-led brand that flips consoles for proof, and turns the audience that
> proof attracts into a **paid monthly community** that teaches people to do the
> same — where the founder is also their guaranteed buyer.

Three engines, one flywheel. The consoles aren't the asset. **The community is.**

## 1. The strategic spine (read this first)

Two facts decide everything:

1. **You have under £1k to trade with.** → You cannot win on inventory. Don't try.
2. **Your content is already hitting the algorithm.** → Attention is the asset you
   actually have. Don't waste it.

Therefore:

- **Trading is the PROOF, not the main income.** Run it lean (broker / pre-sell /
  fast flips). Its job is to generate real results + content, not to be the business.
- **The monthly subscription community is the MAIN income.** Near-zero capital,
  ~95% margin, recurring, scales infinitely. This is where the money lives.
- **Content is the engine** that fills the community. You already started it. This
  is your unfair advantage — most people teaching "flipping" have no audience and
  no real deals. You have both.

**The money goal isn't "flip more consoles." It's "grow MRR."**
100 members × £35/mo = £3,500/mo recurring, whether you flip a single console that
month or not. That's the whole game.

## 3. Engine 1 — The Trading Desk (lean, under £1k)

**Operating rule: recycle cash, never freeze it.** With £1k your enemy is stock that
sits. Aim to turn your capital over 2–4× per month.

### 3.1 The pre-sell / broker mechanic (your main move)

Instead of buy → hope to sell, do **line up buyer → then buy.**

1. Build a **buyer list** (resellers, shops, collectors, CeX-arbitrage guys) who tell
   you "I'll take any PS5 under £X, any Switch under £Y, any retro CIB."
2. When you find a deal, you already know your exit and your margin *before* you spend.
3. For bigger items, take a deposit or sell-on-the-spot so your own £1k barely moves.

This is how you trade £5k+ of consoles a month on £1k of capital.

### 3.2 What to buy at £1k (fast-moving, lower-risk)

- **Nintendo Switch / Switch Lite / OLED** — always liquid, easy spreads.
- **PS4 / Xbox One** — cheap in, steady out, good for volume.
- **Retro, bundled & CIB** (SNES, N64, GameCube, PS1/PS2, Dreamcast, Game Boy) —
  *highest margin per £*, especially complete-in-box + game lots. This is the sweet
  spot for low capital.
- **Faulty consoles you can fix** (HDMI port, disc drive, joy-con drift) — repair =
  margin. If you can solder, this is a cheat code. If not, partner with someone who can.
- **Job lots / bundles** — buy the bundle, break it up, sell the parts for more than
  the whole.

**Avoid at £1k:** holding new-gen flagships long-term (they depreciate), anything slow
to sell, anything you can't price with confidence.

### 3.3 Where to source (UK)

Facebook Marketplace (mispriced + "collection only"), eBay (auctions ending at odd
hours, faulty listings, Buy-It-Now underpriced), car boot sales, Gumtree, Vinted,
Nextdoor, local FB selling groups, house clearances, CeX clearance, Cash Converters.

### 3.4 Where to sell (UK)

Your buyer list (best margin, instant), eBay, Facebook Marketplace, Vinted, console/
retro FB groups, CeX (low price but instant cash to recycle), other resellers. Ship
internationally *later* once cashflow allows.

### 3.5 Target economics (illustrative)

| Item | Buy | Sell | Net spread | Days to flip |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Switch | £110 | £170 | ~£45 after fees | 2–5 |
| PS4 + games | £90 | £150 | ~£45 | 3–7 |
| Retro CIB bundle | £60 | £160 | ~£85 | 5–14 |
| Faulty PS5 (fixed) | £200 | £380 | ~£140 | 5–10 |

Even modestly: 10–15 flips/month on £1k recycled = **£500–£1,200/mo trading profit.**
Nice — but the *content and credibility* it generates is worth more than the cash.

> Every flip is a piece of content. Film the source, the clean-up, the sale, the spread.

## 4. Engine 2 — The Education Ladder (the real money)

A **value ladder**: free → cheap → recurring → premium. Each step warms people up for
the next. The recurring tier is the centre of gravity.

### 4.1 The ladder

| Tier | Product | Price | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0 — Free** | Public content + free broadcast channel / free community | £0 | Capture attention, build trust, collect contacts |
| **1 — Low ticket** | "Console Flipping Starter Playbook" (PDF/mini-course): where to source, how to price, first-flip checklist | £19–29 one-off | Convert follower → buyer (first £ is the hardest) |
| **2 — ⭐ THE SUBSCRIPTION (core)** | Monthly community: weekly deal alerts, sourcing lists, price guides, live Q&A, member chat, templates, your buyer-list access | **£29–49/mo** | **MRR. The whole business.** |
| **3 — 1-to-1 / scale-up** | Private coaching block: I help you scale to £X/mo, personalised | £300–1,000 | High-margin premium, few clients, big ticket |
| **4 — Done-with-you / partner** | I buy your stock / profit-share / you get priority buyer access | rev-share | Ties members to you long-term; feeds Engine 1 |

### 4.2 What the £39/mo community actually contains (so it's worth it & people stay)

Retention is everything in subscriptions. Give them reasons to never cancel:

- **Weekly "live deals" drops** — specific consoles worth buying right now, with the
  numbers. (This alone is worth the fee.)
- **The sourcing vault** — your lists of where/how to find stock.
- **Price guide** — what every console/bundle is really worth this month.
- **Weekly live call** — you flipping live, answering questions, hot seats.
- **Member wins channel** — social proof + dopamine.
- **The buyer's desk** — *you'll buy their stock.* (the killer feature — see §5.)
- **Templates** — listing scripts, negotiation messages, DM openers.

**Platform:** use **Whop** or **Skool** (both built for exactly this — paid community +
recurring billing + content, used heavily by UK reseller communities). Whop is
especially native to the reselling/youth audience. Avoid building your own — YAGNI.

### 4.3 The subscription maths (your MRR path)

| Members | @ £39/mo | Reality |
|---|---|---|
| 10 | £390/mo | Week 1–4. Your founding members, cheap/free to seed. |
| 50 | £1,950/mo | ~Month 2–3 if content keeps hitting. |
| 100 | £3,900/mo | The "I can do this full-time" line. |
| 250 | £9,750/mo | ~6–12 months. Now it's a real business. |
| 500 | £19,500/mo | The asset. This is sellable. |

Churn is the enemy — that's why the community delivers weekly value, not a one-time
course. A course gets refunded and forgotten; a community with live deals gets kept.

## 5. Engine 3 — The Community → Supply Chain Loop (monetise twice)

This is the clever bit and it must be done as a genuine **win-win**, or it becomes
exploitative and the trust dies.

**The loop:**
1. You teach a member to find a console deal (they pay you monthly — money #1).
2. The member finds a console but wants a fast, guaranteed exit / doesn't want the
   hassle of selling. **You buy it off them** at a fair price (you get stock cheap).
3. You sell it to your buyer list at a margin (money #2).
4. The member's win becomes content → attracts more members (money #1 again).

**Why it's a win-win, not a scam:**
- The member gets a *guaranteed instant buyer* — the #1 thing beginners are scared of
  ("what if I can't sell it?"). You remove that fear entirely.
- You get volume sourcing without lifting a finger to hunt.
- Frame it honestly: *"Find deals, and if you don't want the hassle of selling, I'll
  take it off your hands same-day for a fair price. You still profit, I profit, no one's
  stuck."*

**Optional power-ups:**
- **Group buys** — pool member capital for bigger lots, split the profit.
- **Profit-share tier** — you front cash, they do legwork, split the spread.
- **Priority buyer access** as a perk of higher tiers.

This loop is what makes your community *stickier than any competitor's* — nobody else
is the buyer too.

## 6. Engine 4 — The Content Machine (fills everything)

You already started this and it's working. Now make it deliberate.

### 6.1 Content pillars (rotate these)

1. **Deal breakdowns** — "Bought this for £X, sold for £Y, here's the £Z spread."
   (Your bread and butter. People love watching money get made.)
2. **Behind-the-scenes hunts** — car boots, marketplace scrolling, the grind. Raw.
3. **Member/community wins** — "My guy just made £200 on his first flip." Social proof.
4. **Teach-y snippets** — one tip, one mistake, one price insight per video.
5. **You / the story** — the personality, the lifestyle, the "why." People follow
   people. Don't hide behind the brand.

### 6.2 The signature series (make ONE thing your hook)

Pick a flagship format people anticipate. e.g.:
- **"£100 → £1,000 challenge"** — flip a small bankroll up, documented. Insanely
  watchable, ongoing, and *it sells the dream the community delivers.*

### 6.3 Cadence & funnel

- **Post 1–2× daily** on TikTok + Reels (same clip both places). Volume + consistency
  is what your mate meant by "just bang content."
- **Every video drives to the free broadcast channel / free community** ("comment FLIP
  / link in bio"). Don't sell the paid thing cold — capture them free first.
- **Funnel:** viral clip → free channel → low-ticket playbook → £39/mo community →
  1-to-1. Warm them at each step.

### 6.4 OWN your audience (critical)

Algorithms can ban you overnight. Pull followers off-platform into something you
control: a **free Whop/Skool community, a Telegram, or an email list.** If TikTok
vanished tomorrow, your business should survive. This is non-negotiable.

## 8. Funding & cashflow

- **Do NOT raise money. You don't need to.** This is a bootstrap business — that's its
  beauty.
- **Trading capital:** start at £1k, recycle fast, reinvest profit. Use the pre-sell/
  broker model so buyers' money funds bigger deals, not yours.
- **The community funds the trading.** Once MRR > £1k/mo, the subscription cashflow can
  feed your inventory budget — so the business funds its own growth.
- **Reinvest the first ~£2–3k of profit into:** better camera/lighting/mic for content,
  the Whop/Skool subscription, maybe a basic repair kit (soldering, HDMI ports) to
  unlock faulty-console margins.
- **When to consider outside money:** only if you want to hold serious inventory at
  scale (£20k+) — and even then, a buyer-financed / consignment model beats a loan.

## 9. 90-Day execution roadmap

### Weeks 1–2 — Foundation
- Lock brand name + handle. Set up free community (Whop/Skool/Telegram) + email capture.
- Add "comment X / link in bio → free community" CTA to every post.
- Keep banging content daily. Start the signature series (£100→£1k challenge).
- Start building the **buyer list** (DM/talk to resellers, shops, collectors).
- Do 3–5 real flips, film all of them.

### Weeks 3–6 — First money
- Launch the **low-ticket playbook** (£19–29) to the warm free audience.
- Seed the **paid community** with 10 founding members (discounted "founder" rate).
- Run the first weekly live call / deal drop. Deliver hard.
- Hit 20–40 flips total; refine what sells fastest.

### Weeks 7–12 — Recurring engine on
- Push the £39/mo community publicly; goal **50 members (~£2k MRR)**.
- Turn on the **supply loop** — start buying members' stock.
- Open **2–3 one-to-one** spots at £300–500.
- Double down on whatever content format is converting best.
- Set up proper records + register as sole trader for tax (see §10).

**90-day target:** 50 community members (~£2k MRR) + £500–1k/mo trading + first 1-to-1s.
≈ **£3k/mo blended, recurring-led.**

## 10. Risks & how to kill them

| Risk | Kill it by… |
|---|---|
| **Platform ban / algo death** | Own your audience off-platform (email/Telegram/Whop). Post on TikTok *and* Reels *and* YouTube Shorts. |
| **"Guru" trust problem** | Lead with *real, verifiable* numbers and member results. Radical transparency. Never fake it. This is your moat — protect it. |
| **Console market dip / new gen launch** | Trade liquid + retro (less volatile), don't hold flagships long, keep capital recycling. |
| **Member churn** | Weekly live value (deal drops, calls) — a community, not a course. Track churn monthly. |
| **Stuck stock at £1k** | Pre-sell / broker. Buy only with an exit in mind. |
| **Stolen goods / dodgy sellers** | Keep purchase records, get seller details on higher-value buys, trust your gut. |
| **UK tax & legal** | Register as **sole trader** with HMRC once trading regularly; the £1,000 trading allowance covers only the very start; declare income; keep records; consumer-rights/returns apply when you sell as a business. (Get a quick chat with an accountant once MRR is real — cheap insurance.) |
| **You burn out doing everything** | The roadmap sequences it. Community + content first; automate/delegate trading later. |
