# How to photograph and film a console for the shop

One console = 5 photos + 1 short video. Takes about 10 minutes once you've done it a couple of times.

## Before you start

- Use the **same spot every time** — a plain table near a window works best.
- Shoot in **daylight**. No flash, no dark room, no yellow evening light.
- **Wipe the console down first.** Dust and fingerprints show up massively in photos.
- Turn your phone **sideways (landscape)** for every photo and the video.

## The 5 photos (in this order)

1. **Front** — the whole console, straight on.
2. **Back / ports** — turn it around so all the ports and sockets are visible.
3. **Top** — from above, so any marks or scuffs can be seen. Don't hide them — honest photos are the whole point.
4. **Everything laid out** — the console, the controller, the cables, the box (if there is one), all arranged neatly together in one shot. This is the "what you get" photo.
5. **Serial sticker close-up** — get close to the sticker with the serial number so it's readable.

Photo 1 (the front shot) becomes the cover picture in the shop, so make it the best one.

## The test video (30 seconds max)

One take, no editing needed. Film the console doing these four things:

1. **Power on** — press the button, show it turning on.
2. **Home screen** — show it loads to the home screen.
3. **Disc in and out** — put a disc in, show it's accepted, eject it. (Skip this on digital-only consoles.)
4. **Controller connected** — show the controller working, moving around the menu.

Keep it under 30 seconds. If it's longer, it's fine to do it again — shorter is better.

## Upload the video

1. Upload the video to the **CFY YouTube channel**.
2. Set it to **Unlisted** (that's fine — it just means only people with the link can see it).
3. **Copy the video link.**

## Put it all in the admin page

1. Open the admin page on your phone and add (or edit) the unit.
2. Add the 5 photos — the front shot first, it becomes the cover.
3. Paste the **YouTube link** into the "YouTube video URL" box.
4. If there's a **Stripe payment link** for this unit, paste it into the "Stripe Payment Link" box — that's what makes the Buy button appear. No link yet? Leave it blank; buyers can still reserve on WhatsApp.
5. Fill in the rest (price, condition, what's in the box), hit **Publish**, then check the shop page to see it live.
